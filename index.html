Character
Widuri
Tier 1 Operative / Ratu Sunyi

Her real name no longer exists. It was erased when she entered Ratu Sunyi — no record, no identity, no past. She was given the codename Widuri, a Javanese word for a lone star that guides sailors home. The handlers named her that because when they found her, she was the only fixed point in a room full of chaos. She had not moved in three days.

She was 12 when her father, a low-level court official, was murdered by a drug syndicate he refused to work with. A Ratu Sunyi handler found her still sitting beside his body. She had not eaten. She said nothing. She was already waiting.

Cold. Cynical. She has a gaze that mocks her opponents before they understand what is happening. Precise in execution. Privately ritualistic. She meditates before every contract, burns incense, watches TV static. She says it helps her listen to what the room wants. She is not cruel. She is surgical. There is a difference.

World
"A woman in kebaya is invisible to suspicion in Indonesian society. That is the weapon."

Ratu Sunyi — The Silent Queens — is an all-female assassin syndicate operating out of Java. They recruit broken women and train them in pencak silat, bladed weapons, firearms, and poison. Their cover is the oldest cover in the world: domesticity. A woman at a wedding, at a funeral, at an arisan. Nobody looks twice.

The syndicate operates in the shadows of everyday Indonesian life. Jakarta, Surabaya, Makassar. Targets are exclusively drug lords, corrupt officials, and traffickers who buy their way out of justice. Ratu Sunyi is led by a founder known only as Ibu — never seen, only heard.

The city is always there in the background. The Jakarta skyline. The wet pavement. The warung at 2AM. The fluorescent tubes in old buildings that never stop buzzing. This is not a clean world. It is a humid, neon-lit, deeply human world — and somewhere inside it, women in kebaya are doing things nobody will ever be able to explain.

Episodes
Episode 01 / Pilot
Malam Ini

Widuri has one contract tonight: retrieve a sealed envelope from a low-life Jakarta scammer. Killing him wasn't on the brief. She does it anyway, ties him to his chair and sets the room on fire. Three more guards meet her in the corridor on the way out. She shoots all three. She walks out and lights a cigarette. The envelope and its contents are never explained.

Episode 02
The Swamp

Somewhere in a Jametkarta swamp, Widuri is alone on an old wooden boat. She smokes, with an old Javanese song playing from her phone. The same song her father used to hum when she was a kid. Someone is also with her, but not for much longer. As they will then disappear in the mist of the swamp.

About the Project
AI as a filmmaking tool, not a shortcut.

Kebaya Kill-N-Beat$ is an experiment in using generative AI to produce a micro-series with cinematic intent. Every shot is written by hand as a text-to-video prompt — obsessively specific, technically precise, and directed like a real film.

The aesthetic is intentional: handheld DV cam, Blair Witch-style voyeurism, no dialogue, pure sound design and atmosphere. The goal is to see how far AI video generation can go when treated as a camera, not a magic button.

Widuri is the vehicle. Indonesia is the backdrop. The red kebaya is the signature. Everything else is figured out one prompt at a time.
